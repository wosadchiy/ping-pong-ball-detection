Drive Simulator

Pure-JS replica of the firmware velocity-ramp algorithm in cameraControl.ino. Drag the sliders — everything you see is what the Arduino would actually do, computed with the same vel_table[], the same accel_skip, and the same Timer2 ISR walk. No flashing required.

Firmware-side computed

accel_skip = round(1000 · (max_omega / 200) / α), clamped to [1, 250]
αeff = (max_omega / 200) · 1000 / accel_skip
αmax = max_omega · 1000 / 200   (when accel_skip = 1)
vel_table[i] = round(11172 / (max_omega · i / 200))  ·  pulse period in µs
target_idx = clamp(round(target_omega · 200 / max_omega), ±200)

Plots

● ω(t) — velocity ramp

Stepwise advance of cur_v_idx toward target_v_idx, one ±1 step every accel_skip ms. Dashed line = target.

cur_v_idx → ω(user-units) target ω zero line

● Step pulses

Vertical tick = one rising edge on stepPin. Periods T₁, T₂, … shrink as OCR1A drops along the ramp. At idx=0 the motor is muted (no ticks).

step pulse |ω| envelope muted (idx=0)

● T(n) — period of the nth pulse

Period of pulse #n in µs. Steep drop = motor is accelerating (each successive pulse is closer than the previous, exactly like the sketch that motivated all this).

Tn = tn − tn−1 (µs)

Notes & caveats

Quantisation matches firmware. Velocity is discretised into V_TABLE_N = 200 levels in [0, max_omega]. The Timer2 ISR ticks at 1 kHz and advances cur_v_idx by ±1 every accel_skip ticks. Step pulse generation is hardware (Timer1 CTC + COM1A0 toggle); the simulator just walks last_pulse_us += OCR1A within each 1 ms ISR window, which is faithful to within ±1 pulse at OCR1A boundaries.
Rebuild on max_omega. When max_omega changes, the firmware rebuilds vel_table[] and recomputes accel_skip so that the physical α stays constant. The simulator does the same on every input change — no sticky state.
α ceiling. At accel_skip = 1 the ramp is at hardware max: αmax = max_omega · 5 user/sec² (since 200 levels in 1 sec). Asking for more silently clamps. To unlock faster ramps, raise Max Speed.
